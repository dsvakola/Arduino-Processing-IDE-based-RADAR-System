# Code of Conduct

This project is used in an educational environment at **Vidyasagar Academy**.  
All participants are expected to maintain a respectful and supportive atmosphere.

---

## Our Goals

- Help students learn Arduino, sensors, and visualization in a positive way  
- Encourage curiosity, experimentation, and constructive feedback  
- Keep the project safe and welcoming for everyone  

---

## Expected Behaviour

- Be respectful and polite in all discussions and comments.  
- Give feedback that is **helpful and specific**, not insulting.  
- Assume others are learning, just like you.  
- Respect different levels of experience and backgrounds.  

Examples of good behaviour:
- Explaining a better approach instead of just saying “this is wrong”.  
- Asking questions when something is unclear.  
- Crediting others if you build on their ideas or code.  

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviour is not acceptable in issues, discussions, or pull requests:

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks  
- Discriminatory language or jokes  
- Sharing harmful, misleading, or malicious code  
- Spamming or advertising unrelated content  

---

## Reporting Problems

If you feel uncomfortable with someone’s behaviour or see a serious violation:

- You may contact the maintainer privately (for example, through Vidyasagar Academy’s official channels or email, if provided in the repository).  
- Provide as much detail as possible (links, screenshots, etc.).

The maintainer may:
- Ask the person to change their behaviour  
- Remove comments or close discussions that break this Code of Conduct  
- Block repeat offenders from contributing further  

---

## Final Note

This project exists to **support learning**.  
Let us keep it friendly, respectful, and focused on helping each other grow in Electronics, Robotics, and Programming.
